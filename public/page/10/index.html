What remains.

Dec 9, 2010 · 1 minute read
Uncategorized

Of all I have left
that survived the frost,
I cling to my island.
It holds me,
weathered,
as the pressure slips
and I lose my grip.
Fleeting,
fleeting is the warmth
from my fingertips
and toes;
I watch it escape me.
Desparately reaching out
to reclaim what’s mine,
what remains.

View

Nov 9, 2010 · 1 minute read
Uncategorized

I dreamt her once.
I couldn’t see from her perspective
so she lent me her eyes.
And I lost them.
I searched the world,
only to find ones discarded and used.
When I was done
and, downtroddenly, had returned home
she, blind, greeted me warmly.
Alarmed by her smile
I confessed to my crime.
"I couldn’t see
the things you wanted me to.
So I took on your eyes and I saw the world
until I lost them.
I’ve traversed the world
and still they are missing.
You must take mine.”
Her hand grasped my own
"They were given to you
for you desired to see what I do.
That you have acted
proves you have seen.”

Flash Fiction

We let the sun burn our skin. In fact, it had been the furthest thing from our minds to remember sunscreen. The clear, blue sky stretched out from horizon to horizon, uninterrupted by hills or buildings. The yellow grass blended in colour with the thirsty, sand-packed soil that had been baking since sun-up. Our soft patch of grass was a blessing.
I rolled over; my head sliding from his stomach to his chest. Heartbeat. Smile.

He cocked his head to the side and with a smirk asked, “What?”
“Nothing,” I giggled.
“You lie, what is it?” His arm swung out from behind his makeshift pillow of towel and book to brush my forehead.
“I like this.”

small words.

Oct 16, 2010 · 1 minute read
Uncategorized

I’m tired and alive though
it’s hard to survive these
hallows.

It hurts when we cry so
we learn to get by with
silence.

But I don’t want to be the
empty soul you see, not
ever.

I’ll give this a chance ‘cause
I’d never see romance as
shallow.
