Out and About, He's Around

Nov 8, 2008 · 1 minute read
Uncategorized

step.step.stepping
out.
creep.creep.creeping
about.
turn.turn.turning
around.

taking what
belongs to me.
hands
to grab it physically.
fingers sculpt
and part the sea.

branch.branch.branching
out.
spin.spin.spinning
about.
shake.shake.shaking
around.

smiles extend
while muscles play.
scatterbrained thoughts
in daydream purée.
naked toes among
the grass buffet.

burn.burn.burning
out.
heave.heave.heaving
about.
close.close.closing
around.

breathing deep
with fluid sound.
laying back
with feeling profound.
me to be
part of the mound.

go.go.going
out.
breathe.breathe.breathing
about.
lay.lay.laying
around.

At Great Length

Nov 8, 2008 · 2 minute read
Uncategorized

One:
I… 
notice you’re sitting, 
closing your eyes 
and not even listening 
to the words 
coming out of your 
mouth.

You… 
aren’t making this easy, 
I’ve flattered your heart; 
you just make mine queasy 
with your lack 
of true thought and 
response.

Two:
I… 
just can’t stop speaking, 
your words touched a spark 
and here I am, freaking, 
that I’ll 
never tell you how it 
feels.

You… 
are watching, frustrated, 
as I carve out a story 
on a topic outdated 
and cannot 
seem to find my words 
at all.

—

One:
You… 
must not yet be open 
with what’s on your mind, 
and what has been chosen,
so I see 
in-the-twitching 
of your eye.

Two:
I…
am failing so hard, 
all because of 
this heart I must guard; 
this heart 
I have restrained 
for so long.

…but…

you’ll never understand 
why I must do it, 
it’s been shattered before, 
only just made it through it— 
and it cannot be expected
to withstand the pain 
that exists in sweet… 
longing to tell you, 
inside and out,
give you a glimpse 
of what’s hanging about 
in all I say and do. 
Does that matter to you, 
can you even see me fighting within, 
trying to win, 
wanting to touch you, 
and still I, 
am without the strength?

Interdelusions

Nov 8, 2008 · 1 minute read
Uncategorized

The swelling orange
on the apple tree,
was plucked in the snow.

The leathery skin
of a newborn child,
with naught to grow.

The English alphabet accents
create all the words
that the mute man speaks.

The sight of wind
falling to the ground
in groans and creaks.
